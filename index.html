COSMOS: TV Series 3 172 Tyson, Science, NASA, NYC, Carl Sagan Neil DeGrasse Tyson is a leading American astrophysicist. He studied first at Harvard and then at Columbia University. He has written several books, including Origins: Fourteen Billion Years of Cosmic Evolution, which was based on Origins which was a two part television series he hosted on NOVA. He has been awarded the NASA Distinguished Public Service Medal for his work with NASA. He is an opponent of intelligent design and is the director of the Hayden Planetarium at the American Museum of Natural History in New York City. One of his main goals has been to make science accessible to the every day person and to inspire a renewed interest in science and space exploration. His most infamous trait is that he was a proponent of the removal of Pluto as a planet and headed the effort to remove it. He has been greatly influenced by Carl Sagan and thinks of him as an inspiration, even resurrecting Sagan's television show Cosmos. He has had several honorary titles bestowed upon him, including People Magazine's "Sexiest Scientist Alive" in 2000.
